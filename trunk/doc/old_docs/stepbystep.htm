Step-by-Step run of system:

 

console: type ant run.gui to start the system

 

-   type in user name: ‘eric’, password ‘e’, position: ‘supervisor’ and login

-   click on a name in the list on the left side of the screen

-   feel free to change any information (except username, which cannot be edited) and click ‘submit edit’.  Changes will be effective immediately.

-   Click ‘clear form’ and add an employee, choosing which types of air traffic controller they can login as

-   Feel free to edit and delete employees at will

-   Click on ‘Local Controller’ tab, and press ‘Start Scenario’ button.

-   Click next time step a couple times to advance the scenario

-   Click anywhere on the map portion of the screen to display the names of the planes.

-   Click on the risk “collision between plane d901 and plane d900.” This will populate a list of mitigations. Click on mitigation “Move Plane D900” and then click on action “Turn D900 Left at Int R1 and T2.” This should be the first option, as it is the closest intersection to the plane. Then click “execute action”

-   Advance the scenario by clicking ‘next time step’ until the plane on the left side of the screen turns on taxiway marked T2

-   Now Right – click on Plane D901 (the plane on the right side of the screen)

-   In the popup menu, choose the option ‘turn D901 right at int. R1 and T4”

-   Now click the ‘Ground control’ tab.  You’ll notice that local control can only control planes when they are on the runway, and ground control can only control planes when on taxiways.

-   Once in the ground control tab click ‘Start Scenario’ button (a known bug), and then right-click on D900 (on the left of the screen) and choose option ‘Turn left at int. Terminal and T2’ from the resulting popup menu

-   Advance the scenario until plane makes the turn

-   Right click again on the same plane and make it turn left at int. Terminal and T1.

-   Advance scenario until plane turns.  D901 (on the right of the screen) should be turning onto T4 at about the same time.

-   Using the right click function, tell D900 (the left plane) to turn left at T1 and R1, and tell D901 to turn left at T4 and Terminal.  Then tell D901 to Stop

-   Advance timesteps until D900 is on the runway again. Change back to the local control tab.

-   Using Right click, tell D900 to Takeoff and advance the scenario until the plane is off the screen.

-   Now switch back to Ground Control tab and using right click tell the plane to speed up (about three or four times).  Note that in an earlier step you have already told the plane to turn left at int T4 and Terminal.

-   Still using the right click method, tell plane to turn left at Terminal and T1 and then once that turn has been made, turn plane left again at T1 and R1.

-   Switch back to local control and have plane takeoff.

-   Finally, click on the weather icon in the top right of the screen and choose an option from the popup menu.  This will change the weather on the next timeclick and you can see the result in the cooresponding weather risk.

-   This ends the planned simulation.

 

Obviously with our dynamic system, there are endless possibilities.  This is just one suggested route that demonstrates our functionality, but feel free to play around with it in a different way.

 

You can log out and log back in at any time to restart the simulation.

 

Note: do not let the planes reach the end of a runway or taxiway without giving it a new direction.  The plane would continue on and be lost to you.  This would be changed for a later submission, and A* would help this micromanagement problem out immensely.  Also, in the final system, the right click functionality would be replaced with voice recognition software.
